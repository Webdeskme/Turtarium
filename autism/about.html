🧠 What Is Autism?

Autism, also called Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), is a way some people’s brains work. It's not an illness — it just means your brain processes things like sounds, feelings, and conversations differently.

People with autism might:

  • 🗣️ Have a hard time talking with others or keeping a conversation going
  • 🧍‍♂️🤝 Struggle to make or keep friendships
  • 👀 Find it difficult to read body language, facial expressions, or tone of voice
  • 🔁 Do the same things again and again (repeating movements or phrases)
  • 👂👃 Be sensitive to lights, sounds, smells, or textures
  • 🕓 Feel upset when routines or plans suddenly change
  • 🧩 Have delayed language or different ways of learning and thinking

If a few of these sound like you, you might be autistic. That’s okay! Knowing can help you understand yourself better — and help others support you in the way that works for your brain.

🧭 What to Do If You Think You Might Be Autistic

✅ Step 1: Talk to a Doctor

Start by telling your doctor or a trusted adult (like a teacher, parent, or caregiver) how you feel. Ask if they can help you talk to a professional like:

  • A psychologist
  • A psychiatrist
  • A neurologist

These people are trained to help diagnose autism and understand how your brain works.

🧪 Step 2: Get an Evaluation

An autism evaluation might include:

  • Watching how you act and talk
  • Asking about your childhood
  • Testing how you think, solve problems, and use language
  • Talking about how you handle daily life tasks

Some people also get a neuropsychological evaluation, which helps figure out:

  • 🧠 How you learn
  • 🧩 How you solve problems
  • 🧘 How you handle stress and emotions
  • 🗂️ How you plan and organize

This helps create a plan that supports your brain and learning style.

📍 Where to Get Evaluated in Rhode Island

Here are trusted places in Rhode Island that can help with autism evaluations:

  • Bradley Hospital – East Providence, RI
  • Butler Hospital – Providence, RI
  • Rhode Island Hospital – Providence, RI
  • The Autism Project – Warwick, RI

🧱 What to Do While You Wait

  • 🧑‍💻 Learn more about autism at Autism Self Advocacy Network
  • 🤝 Join a community like Wrong Planet to meet others like you
  • 📚 Read or watch stories about autistic people to hear real experiences
  • 🗣️ Talk to someone you trust

🧭 What to Do After a Diagnosis

Getting a diagnosis can feel like a big deal — but it’s a good step! Here’s what you can do next:

🧠 Build Your Support Network

  • Talk to friends, family, or mentors who care about you
  • Find a therapist or counselor if you want help with emotions or anxiety
  • Ask for workplace or school support if you need it

🧰 Get Tools and Support

  • Ask about accommodations at school or work
  • Find helpful tools like planners, noise-canceling headphones, or fidgets
  • Check out The Autism Project Rhode Island

💬 Learn to Advocate for Yourself

You have the right to ask for what you need. It’s okay to say:

"I need extra time on this."
"Bright lights hurt my eyes."
"I get overwhelmed by noise."

❤️ You Are Not Alone

Autism is part of who you are. It can bring strengths like:

  • Great memory
  • Unique creativity
  • Strong focus on things you love
  • Honest and clear communication

Asking for help doesn’t make you weak — it makes you smart.
