Social Skills & The Community

Social Skills

Do you ever feel overwhelmed in social situations? Maybe the lights are too bright, the noise is too loud, or you're just not sure what to do. Well, today we're going to learn a simple trick to help you feel more comfortable and confident in social situations. It's called S.E.A.

Supplement

The "S" in S.E.A stands for Supplement. This means that if you need something to help you feel more comfortable, you can use a supplement. For example, if the room is too bright, you can wear sunglasses. If it's too loud, you can wear earplugs. And if it's too cold, you can wear a sweater. It's like having a special tool to help you feel better.

Exit

The "E" in S.E.A stands for Exit. If you're feeling really overwhelmed and you can't use a supplement to help, it's okay to exit the situation. This means you can quietly get up and leave the room. It's like taking a break to recharge and come back when you're feeling better.

Ask

The "A" in S.E.A stands for Ask. You can always ask for for help. (example: "Could you turn down the lights?")

If you're not sure what to do in a social situation, you can ask them a question about themselves, like "What's your favorite hobby?" or "What do you like to do for fun?" This can help you feel more comfortable and start a conversation.

Example scenario

Let's say you're at a birthday party and it's really loud. You can use your earplugs (Supplement) to block out the noise. But if it's still too much, you can quietly get up and leave the room (Exit), or you could ask if the music could be turned down (Ask). And if you're not sure what to do once you are more compfortable at the party, you can ask someone a question, like "What's your favorite game?" (Ask).

Conclusion

So, remember the S.E.A acronym: Supplement, Exit, and Ask. It's like having a special toolbox to help you feel more comfortable and confident in social situations. Just remember to use your supplements, exit when you need to, and ask for help when you're not sure what to do. You got this!
